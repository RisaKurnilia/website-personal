      Nurturing Literacy in Young Minds: A Rewarding Experience, Collaboration TSA USU with Tanoto Foundation Medan 

                Among the projects I participated in was the "Literacy Culture Enhancement Training for Teachers and Students at SD Yabes Medan," a collaboration with Tanoto Foundation Medan (2023). This project provided me with the opportunity to directly engage in enhancing literacy culture among elementary school students for over three months, with activities taking place every Saturday. During the initial phase, I and my fellow facilitators, along with the teachers, received training from the Tanoto Foundation on effective communication and interaction with children aged 8-11 years old. We were also introduced to various learning modules that could promote literacy culture, such as reading, writing, storytelling, and more. A few weeks later, my team and I created our own learning model that fostered children's interest in literacy.

           Initially, I felt awkward interacting with them as I rarely spoke with children much younger than me. However, thanks to the supportive environment and my fellow facilitators who provided guidance and examples, I was able to adapt well and began to feel comfortable working within the team. While it was sometimes tiring to keep up with the energy of active and playful children, I started to experience the joy of sharing knowledge with them and playing together.

           This project provided me with a wealth of new experiences: learning how to interact with children, expanding my social circle, and feeling a sense of fulfillment from directly contributing to the quality of education for the nation's youth. On the last day of the program, I received handmade gifts from some of my students. This project holds immense value for me as it allowed me to directly interact with them for an extended period, creating a positive and lasting impression on both sides
